Sketch narrative

This sketch began with the idea of making a countdown that feels relaxed rather than rigid for studying. I experimented with metaphors that communicate time going away, and settled on sticky notes slowly being added to a board. After feedback, however, I decided it would be better to have the sticky notes fall off from the board. The design focuses on smooth motion and soft colors to create a sense of calm for study or creative breaks. Iterating on color and spacing helped balance realism with legibility while avoiding clutter. The main challenge was timing each note’s fall to keep the movement natural yet readable. Overall, this design succeeds in representing short spans of time in a friendly, approachable way. If developed further, I would explore adding interactivity such as clicking notes to mark completed tasks or adjusting duration for different sessions.

Sketch Evolution

Sketch narrative

This clock translates the Pomodoro routine into a water level that rises during study and falls during breaks. My goal was to show progress through a single continuous shape rather than discrete segments. Early versions used multiple bars, but the single container proved cleaner and easier to read. I refined the motion curve to make filling and draining smooth, giving the clock a steady rhythm that matches focused work. The minimal color palette and subtle text labels make it clear which phase is active without distraction. Overall, the design feels balanced and meditative, fitting for students who want a visual timer that communicates time passing quietly. Future improvements could include pause controls, custom durations, or gentle sound cues.

Final Clock Narrative & Evolution

This clock uses a ring of pastel dots that glow in sequence to mark the passing seconds while a centered panel shows the Pomodoro phase and a clear mm:ss countdown. I tested segmented arcs and radial fills, then chose discrete dots with a soft glow because the motion reads cleanly at a glance and stays calm. Early palettes felt too bright, so I shifted to cooler blues and pinks with slight jitter to avoid flat repetition. The sweep uses eased falloff to keep the highlight smooth and the dot size grows subtly near the active position for added focus. The result balances ambience and legibility for study sessions. It is easy to read without pulling attention away from work. Next steps could add adjustable durations, pause and resume, and a gentle sound option.
